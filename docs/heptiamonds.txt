Four 2x3 Hexagons
=================

In various configurations.

1. Four 2x3 hexagons stacked vertically: `solutions incomplete
   <../solutions/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-1.txt>`__

   .. image:: images/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-1.png

2. Two adjacent groups of two 2x3 hexagons stacked vertically:
   `solutions incomplete
   <../solutions/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-2.txt>`__

   .. image:: images/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-2.png

3. Four 2x3 hexagons bunched together (one nestled on each side of a
   central stack of two; similar to O4 tetrahex): `solutions incomplete
   <../solutions/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-3.txt>`__

   .. image:: images/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-3.png

4. As in #3, but the two central hexagons are now two vertical units
   apart: `solutions incomplete
   <../solutions/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-4.txt>`__

   .. image:: images/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-4.png

5. As in #3, but the two central hexagons are now four vertical units
   apart: `solutions incomplete
   <../solutions/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-5.txt>`__

   .. image:: images/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-5.png

6. Four 2x3 hexagons arranged as a trefoil (similar to Y4 tetrahex):
   `solutions incomplete
   <../solutions/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-6.txt>`__

   .. image:: images/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-6.png

7. Four 2x3 hexagons adjacent horizontally, with corners touching:
   `solutions incomplete
   <../solutions/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-7.txt>`__

   .. image:: images/heptiamonds-hexagons-2x3-7.png
